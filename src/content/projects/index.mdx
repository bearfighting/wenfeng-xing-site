## Why I Build

I do not build products to collect projects.

I build tools that I want to keep using ten years from now.

Most of them begin as personal needs, become software I rely on, and then gradually evolve into public tools, open-source libraries, or products that others can use too.

This page is not a portfolio in the traditional sense.

It is a builder's lab: a place to track long-term work, research directions, public code, and ideas that are still early but worth preserving.
